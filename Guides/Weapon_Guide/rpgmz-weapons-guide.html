Complete Guide · No Plugins · Explained Like You're Five

The Weapons Database — Big Picture

Think of a weapon entry as a card with three zones: basic info at the top, number boosts in the middle (Params), and special rules at the bottom (Traits). Together they define everything a weapon does.

🗂 The Three Zones of a Weapon
TOP ZONE
Basic Info

Name, icon, description, Weapon Type (sword, bow, etc.), Animation (what plays on attack), and Price / Equip type. These are mostly labelling fields — they don't directly affect combat math except for Weapon Type, which determines which actors can equip it.

MIDDLE ZONE
Parameters (Params)

Eight stat bonuses that get added directly to the actor's base stats when the weapon is equipped. If your character has 40 ATK and the sword gives +20 ATK, they now have 60 ATK. Simple addition.

BOTTOM ZONE
Traits

These are rules and modifiers — not flat numbers, but percentage multipliers, resistances, special attack effects, and behavioral changes. Traits are where the real power lies and where most confusion happens.

🔑 Key Concept

Params are flat math. Traits are rules. A weapon can give you +50 ATK (Param) AND deal Fire damage (Trait) at the same time — they do completely different things.

Weapon Type — What Does It Do?

The Weapon Type field (Sword, Axe, Bow, etc.) does two things:

1. Equip Restriction
In each Actor's settings, you define which weapon types they can equip. A mage locked to "Staves" can't equip a sword — even if you give it mage stats.
2. Skill Gating
Skills can require a specific weapon type to use. A "Sword Arts" skill can check "is the actor equipping a Sword type?" — if not, the skill is greyed out. You set this in the Skill's required weapon type field.
✅ Beginner Tip

The Weapon Type names ("Sword", "Axe"…) are just labels you define in the System tab. You can rename them to anything. MZ doesn't care what you call them — it just matches the ID number.

Parameters (Params)

These are the eight flat number bonuses on a weapon. They add directly to the actor's stats. Nothing fancy — just addition.

MHP
Max Hit Points
Boosts the wearer's maximum HP. A shield that gives +500 MHP means they can take 500 more damage before dying.
+200 MHP → actor goes from 800 HP to 1000 HP
MMP
Max Magic Points
Boosts maximum MP. Useful on staves/accessories for mages who need more spell casts per battle.
+100 MMP → can cast more spells before running dry
ATK
Attack Power
Physical attack strength. Used in the standard damage formula for physical skills. The bigger this is, the harder physical hits land.
Default formula: (a.atk * 4 - b.def * 2)
DEF
Defense Power
Physical damage reduction. In the default formula, DEF reduces incoming physical damage. Heavy armor stacks this.
High DEF = absorbs more physical hits
MAT
Magic Attack Power
Magic spell strength. Same role as ATK but for magical skills. Staves and mage weapons boost this.
Default formula: (a.mat * 4 - b.mdf * 2)
MDF
Magic Defense Power
Reduces incoming magic damage. The magical equivalent of DEF. Robes and magic-resist gear stacks this.
High MDF = tanky against spells
AGI
Agility
Controls turn order. Higher AGI = acts first in combat. Note: In MZ, AGI does not feed into HIT or EVA by default — those are separate X-Params (see Traits tab). Agility only determines action order.
Acts before slower enemies if AGI is higher
LUK
Luck
Affects status effect accuracy and resistance, critical rates, and other luck-based rolls. Often underused by beginners but very useful.
High LUK = better crit chance & status resistance
ℹ️ How Params Stack

All param bonuses from all equipped items are added together, then added to the actor's base stat. Equip a sword (+30 ATK) and a ring (+10 ATK) and you get +40 ATK total. Dead simple.

⚠️ Params vs Traits — Don't Confuse These!

Params are flat additions (+20 ATK). Traits include things like "ATK Rate ×150%" which is a percentage multiplier applied to the final stat — a completely different beast. We cover that in the Traits tab.

Traits — The Rules Engine

Traits are NOT flat numbers. They are percentage-based rules, resistances, flags, and modifiers. Think of them like "special laws" applied to the actor while the weapon is equipped.

🗝 The Golden Rule of Traits

Traits are organized into groups. Within each group, they work differently — some multiply together, some add together, some override. You need to know which group you're in.

Group 1 — RATE Traits (Percentage Multipliers)

These multiply an existing stat or resistance by a percentage. 100% = no change. 150% = 1.5× boost. 50% = half.

150%
Boosted (50% more)
100%
Default (no change)
50%
Halved (50% less)
0%
Immune / Nullified
Element Rate
Elemental Resistance / Weakness

How much damage this element deals to the bearer. 200% = double damage (weakness). 50% = half damage (resist). 0% = immune. You can even go -100% to absorb the element as healing.
Example: A Fire Robe with Fire Rate 50% means fire spells do half damage to whoever wears it.

Debuff Rate
How Easily Debuffs Land on the Wearer

This controls how susceptible the wearer is to having their stats debuffed. 200% = debuffs land more easily. 0% = completely immune to debuffs on that stat. This is about being debuffed, not debuffing others.

State Rate
Status Effect Susceptibility

How easily a specific status (Poison, Sleep, Paralysis…) lands on the wearer. 200% = twice as likely to get poisoned. 0% = immune to that status. A charm immunity ring would set the Charm State Rate to 0%.

State Resist
Hard Status Immunity

Unlike State Rate, this is a flat "cannot get this status" switch. If State Resist for Poison is checked, the wearer simply cannot be poisoned, period. No percentage — just on/off immunity.

Group 2 — PARAM Rate Traits (Stat Percentage Boosts)

Parameter Rate
Multiply a Stat by a Percentage

Choose a stat (ATK, DEF, MAT…) and a percentage. This multiplies the actor's total stat by that amount.
Example: Actor has 100 ATK. Weapon has ATK Rate 150%. Final ATK = 150. Stack two items with ATK Rate 150% and they multiply together: 100 × 1.5 × 1.5 = 225.

⚠️ Param Rates MULTIPLY Together

Two weapons giving ATK Rate 150% each don't give +100% ATK. They multiply: 1.5 × 1.5 = 2.25× your ATK. This matters a lot for balance.

Group 3 — X-PARAM Traits (Extra Parameters)

These are additional stats that add together across all sources, and represent combat rates and special abilities. Their default is 0% (not 100% like normal params).

HIT
Hit Rate

Base accuracy of physical attacks. Default is 95% (for actors). Add +5% HIT from a weapon to reach 100%. Missing can be annoying — invest in this for reliable accuracy.

EVA
Evasion Rate

Chance to dodge physical attacks. Default 0%. Adding 20% EVA means 20% of physical attacks miss entirely. Stacks additively with other EVA sources.

CRI
Critical Hit Rate

Chance to land a critical hit (default 3× damage in MZ). Default is 4%. Add +10% CRI on a dagger to make it feel "stabby" and crit-happy. Stacks additively.

CEV
Critical Evasion Rate

Chance to negate an incoming critical hit. Reduces crits landing on the wearer. Good for defensive armor.

MEV
Magic Evasion Rate

Chance to dodge magical skills entirely. The magical version of EVA. Useful on late-game robes or rings.

MRF
Magic Reflection Rate

Chance that a magic skill is reflected back at the caster. 30% MRF means 30% of magic aimed at the wearer hits the attacker instead. Very powerful.

CNT
Counter Attack Rate

When the wearer is hit by a physical attack, this is the chance they automatically counter-attack. A spiky shield with 30% CNT can punish melee enemies automatically.

HRG
HP Regeneration

Percentage of max HP recovered per turn. 5% HRG on a ring means recovering 5% of your max HP at the end of every turn, passively. Great for tanky builds.

MRG
MP Regeneration

Percentage of max MP recovered per turn. Staves with MP regen keep mages fueled.

TRG
TP Regeneration

Percentage of max TP recovered per turn. Useful if your game uses TP heavily.

Group 4 — S-PARAM Traits (Special Parameters)

These also start at 100% by default and multiply together across sources. They control special combat scaling factors.

TGR
Target Rate

How likely enemies are to target this actor. Default 100%. Set to 200% on a heavy knight to make enemies focus them. Set to 0% to make an actor essentially invisible to enemies. Great for tank/aggro mechanics.

GRD
Guard Effect Rate

Multiplies how effective the Guard command is for this actor. Default 100% → guard halves damage. 200% → guard reduces damage more aggressively.

REC
Recovery Effect Rate

Multiplies how much healing this actor receives. 150% REC means healing spells restore 1.5× as much HP. 50% means they're harder to heal. Doesn't affect self-cast heal skills unless you're also the target.

PHA
Pharmacology

Multiplies the effect of items (potions, etc.) used on or by this actor. Default 100%. 200% PHA = potions heal twice as much. Great for a "Chemist" archetype.

MCR
MP Cost Rate

Multiplies the MP cost of all skills for this actor. 50% MCR = all spells cost half MP. Very powerful when stacked — 50% × 50% = 25% of original cost. Great for a "Spell Saver" ring.

TCR
TP Charge Rate

Multiplies how fast TP fills up when taking actions or damage. 200% TCR = TP builds twice as fast.

PDR
Physical Damage Rate

Multiplies all physical damage received. 50% PDR = physical hits only do half. 200% PDR = extremely vulnerable to physical. This is a global physical resistance/weakness.

MDR
Magic Damage Rate

Same as PDR but for magical damage. 50% MDR = magic spells deal half damage to wearer. Stack on a mystic robe for a true mage-resistant character.

FDR
Floor Damage Rate

Multiplies damage taken from floor damage tiles. 0% FDR = completely immune to floor hazards. Mostly niche, useful for dungeon-crawling games with spike floors etc.

EXR
Experience Rate

Multiplies EXP gained by this actor. 150% EXR = this actor levels up faster. Great for an "EXP Charm" accessory.

Group 5 — ATTACK Traits

Attack Element
Add an Element to Normal Attacks

This makes the actor's normal attacks (the "Attack" command) deal damage of the specified element. Stack multiple to hit multiple elements at once. Equip a Fire Sword with Attack Element: Fire and all normal attacks check fire resistance on the enemy.

Attack State
Chance to Apply a Status on Hit

Normal attacks have an X% chance to apply a status effect. Set "Attack State: Poison, 30%" and every normal attack has a 30% chance to poison the target. This is one of the most fun weapon traits for flavor.

Attack Speed
Flat Bonus/Penalty to Action Speed

A flat number added to turn order priority. Positive = acts earlier. Negative = acts later. A "Heavy Greatsword" might have Attack Speed -5 to feel slow and weighty.

Attack Times
Extra Hits Per Normal Attack

Each point adds one additional hit to the normal Attack command. Attack Times +1 = attacks twice. Attack Times +2 = attacks three times. Each hit is calculated separately, so each can crit individually. Very powerful — don't go overboard.

Attack Skill
Replace the Default Attack With a Skill

The "Attack" command uses a skill instead of the basic attack. If you set Attack Skill to "Fire Slash," the Attack button now does Fire Slash. The actor doesn't need to have learned the skill — the weapon grants it automatically.

Group 6 — SKILL / PARTY Traits (Misc)

Add Skill Type
Unlock a Whole Category of Skills

Grants access to all skills belonging to a skill type. Equip a holy staff that has "Add Skill Type: Holy Magic" and the actor can suddenly use all Holy Magic spells — even without learning them individually.

Add Skill
Grant a Specific Individual Skill

Equipping this item grants one specific skill. Like a "Slime Ring" that grants the Slimeball skill while worn. The skill disappears when the item is removed.

Seal Skill Type
Block an Entire Skill Category

Prevents the actor from using any skill in a category. A cursed weapon that seals "Magic" skill type means the actor can't cast any spells while it's equipped.

Seal Skill
Block One Specific Skill

Prevents using a single named skill. More surgical than Seal Skill Type.

Equip Weapon
Allow Equipping a Weapon Type

Grants the ability to equip a weapon type the actor normally can't. A passive that lets your mage equip swords, for example.

Equip Armor
Allow Equipping an Armor Type

Same but for armor types. Let your rogue wear heavy armor if you want.

Slot Type
Dual Wield

When set to "Dual Wield," the actor's Shield slot becomes a second Weapon slot. The actor can now equip two weapons simultaneously. Each weapon's traits and params both apply.

Action Times+
Chance of an Extra Turn

Each turn, there's an X% chance the actor gets an additional action. 25% Action Times+ means a 1-in-4 chance of acting twice in a row. This is an extremely powerful trait — use sparingly.

Special Flag
Auto Battle / Guard / Substitute / Preserve TP

Four binary switches: Auto Battle (AI controls the actor), Guard (always guarding), Substitute (intercepts lethal hits aimed at allies with low HP), Preserve TP (TP doesn't reset to 0 at battle start).

Party Ability
Whole-Party Benefits While Equipped

Grants a party-wide passive as long as ONE party member has this equipped: Encounter Half (50% fewer random battles), Encounter None (no random battles), Cancel Surprise, Raise Preemptive, Gold Double, Item Double. Very useful for exploration-focused accessories.

Buffs, Debuffs & "Who Gets Them?"

This is the most confusing part for beginners. Let's break it down completely.

🔑 The Most Important Rule

In RPG Maker MZ, Buffs and Debuffs in Skills/Traits apply to the TARGET of the skill — not the caster. But the target depends on the skill's scope. Let's walk through it.

Scenario: You put "M.ATK Down" on a weapon trait — what happens?

⚠️ Weapon Traits Don't Have Debuff Effects!

Here's the thing — weapons don't have a "apply buff/debuff" trait. That kind of effect lives on Skills and States (Status Effects), not weapon traits. Weapons have "Attack State" which can apply a STATUS EFFECT on hit — and that status effect can then apply a buff/debuff. Let's trace the full chain.

The Full Chain: How "Attack lowers M.ATK" Works

So the flow is: Weapon → triggers State on target → State lowers enemy's stat. The debuff is on the enemy, not you.

✅ Simple Rule

Ask yourself: "Who is the scope of this skill targeting?" That's who gets the buff or debuff. It doesn't matter who is casting it. The SCOPE (set in the skill's Scope field) determines the target, and the target gets the effect.

What Do Buff/Debuff Actually Do to a Stat?

In MZ, buffs and debuffs work in stacks on a specific stat. Each stat can be buffed or debuffed up to 2 stacks in each direction.

+1 Buff

0 (none)
Stat × 1.0 (normal)
-1 Debuff
Stat × 0.75 (25% less)

ℹ️ Buff Stacking

Adding a buff twice on the same stat goes to +2 stacks max (2.0×). You cannot exceed +2 or go below -2 stacks. Applying more beyond the cap just refreshes the timer. Buffs last for a set number of turns (default 5 turns) then disappear automatically.

The "Add Buff" vs "Debuff Rate" Confusion

🔍 These Are TWO Different Things
Add Buff
(Skill Effect)
Temporarily multiplies a stat for a few turns

This is a temporary in-battle buff. It adds 1 or 2 stacks to a stat, boosting it by 1.5× or 2.0×. Goes away after a set number of turns. Found in the Effects tab of a Skill.

Debuff Rate
(Item Trait)
Changes how susceptible YOU ARE to receiving debuffs

This is a permanent passive on the item wearer. If an enemy tries to debuff your ATK, this percentage changes how likely that debuff lands on you. 0% Debuff Rate (ATK) = immune to ATK debuffs. Found in the Traits tab of a weapon/armor.

Practical Example: "I want enemies to hit less hard after I attack them"

Step-by-Step Setup

Step 1: Create a State called "ATK Down" in the States database. Give it a trait: Parameter Rate → ATK → 75% (25% reduction). Set its auto-removal to "Action End: 2 turns."

Step 2: Go to your weapon in the Weapons database. Add Trait → Attack State → select "ATK Down" → set probability 40%.

Result: Every time the actor using this weapon makes a normal attack, there is a 40% chance the enemy gets the ATK Down state, reducing their ATK by 25% for 2 turns.

✅ The debuff is on the ENEMY

The ATK Down state is on the enemy, reducing the enemy's ATK. The attacker is unaffected. The weapon triggered it via "Attack State."

Elements — Damage Types

Elements are damage types like Fire, Ice, Thunder. They're defined in your System settings and referenced by ID number in the database.

How Elements Work in Weapons

Two Ways to Give a Weapon an Element
Via Skill
The Skill Has the Element

Most of the time, the skill the weapon triggers has the element, not the weapon itself. A "Fire Slash" skill has its damage type set to Fire in the skill's Damage → Type → Element field. The weapon just enables or uses that skill.

Via Trait
Attack Element on the Weapon

Add Trait → Attack → Attack Element → Fire. Now the actor's basic Attack command deals Fire damage in addition to physical. This works on top of anything else. Stack "Attack Element: Fire" and "Attack Element: Ice" on the same weapon for a dual-element attack.

Element Resistance — Element Rate Trait

When you put Element Rate on a weapon/armor, you're telling the game: "While this item is equipped, change how much [element] damage this actor takes."

Weakness (double damage)
100%
Neutral (normal damage)
50%

0%

-100%

ℹ️ Multiple Element Rate Sources

If an actor has two items both modifying Fire Rate, they multiply together. Fire Robe (50% fire rate) + Ring of Fire (50% fire rate) = 25% fire rate. The actor takes only a quarter fire damage. Stack carefully!

✅ Absorb Trick

Setting Element Rate to a negative value (like -100%) means the actor is HEALED by that element instead of damaged. A fire golem with Fire Rate -100% gets healed by fire spells. Use this for unique boss gimmicks or endgame equipment.

Damage Calculation — How It All Fits Together

Understanding the default damage formula helps you make sense of why ATK, DEF, MAT, and MDF matter and how weapon params interact with skills.

The Default Physical Damage Formula

a.atk × 4 − b.def × 2

a = attacker (the one using the skill). b = target (the one being hit). So: (Attacker's total ATK × 4) minus (Target's total DEF × 2).

The Default Magic Damage Formula

a.mat × 4 − b.mdf × 2

Identical structure, but uses MAT and MDF. This is why mage weapons boost MAT and magic armor boosts MDF.

The Full Damage Chain (Simplified)

1. Calculate raw damage from formula (e.g. a.atk × 4 − b.def × 2) — Buff/Debuff multipliers are already baked into a.atk, b.def etc. at this step
↓
2. Apply Element Rate (if elemental — multiply by target's element rate for that element)
↓
3. Apply PDR or MDR (target's physical/magic damage rate — from S-Params)
↓
4. Critical hit check (base 3× damage if CRI roll succeeds) — happens before variance
↓
5. Apply Variance (random roll within ±variance%)
↓
6. Apply Guard reduction (if target is guarding — reduced by GRD rate)
↓
7. Final HP is reduced. Apply any on-hit states (Attack State, Add State in skill effects)
✅ Key Takeaway for Weapon Building

Your weapon's ATK param feeds directly into step 1. Your weapon's Attack Element trait triggers step 2. Your weapon's Attack State trait triggers step 7. Knowing which step each thing affects lets you design smarter weapons.

ℹ️ You Can Edit the Formula

Every skill has a custom formula box. You can write a.atk * 8 - b.def * 2 for a hard-hitting skill, or (a.atk + a.mat) * 2 for a hybrid attack. No plugins needed — just edit the formula field in the skill.
